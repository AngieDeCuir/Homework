How To Potty Train A Puppy

Written By Angie DeCuir

What you need to start potty training

  1. A puppy
  2. Treats
  3. Leash and harness
  4. Puppy pads
  5. Consistency and lots of patience

Getting a puppy is a wonderful addition to a family. Having a pet helps children learn responsibility. There have been studies showing that petting a dog can lower blood pressure. These are all wonderful reasons for getting a puppy. There are some things that need to be started in the beginning. That is were this article can help with potty training your puppy. Potty training takes a lot of consistency and patience, but it will pay off in the long run.Being able to help your puppy learn that they go to the bathroom outside and not on the new rug in the living room will help everyone feel better.

Supplies for when accidents happen

  • Paper towels
  • Disposable Gloves
  • Disinfectant (such as Lysol wipes)
  • Plastic grocery bag to put everything in after cleaning

There are many books and articles online about potty training. The most important thing is being consistent with your puppy. It will take some time for them to get use to a new home, new family members, and other pets. Have patience and understanding when you are potty training. Schedue set times so that your puppy knows what to expect. Give your puppy lots of praise when they get it right. This will encourage them to go outside and use the restroom. Limiting water in the evening can help prevent your puppy needing to go out at 2 am. Also, talking to your vet is always a good idea. Sometimes, frequent urination may mean health issues for your puppy that may need some attention. Keep accident supplies close and try to interrup the stream by saying "Hey" and clapping your hands. When they have stopped scoop up and take them outside and encourage them and praise them for going outside.
